# Pitch Notes

## Hook

We did not make a war game. We made a game about one parent trying to keep two children calm through the last dark watch before dawn. It runs entirely in the browser. Everything in the game is ordinary. The fear is not.

## Demo Walkthrough

You begin inside a small apartment with a phone at low battery. The TV flickers. The radio says something different. A phone message says something else again. Then the younger child appears and asks if everything is okay. Soon after, the power cuts. You run to the kitchen in the dark, trying to find a candle before the phone dies. The moment the candle is lit, someone knocks at the front door. The whole game is built out of short distances, ordinary objects, and decisions that feel heavier than they should.

## Theme

The theme is beauty in the ordinary. A wall clock. A cold cup of tea. A child asking a question you do not know how to answer. The game tries to make those familiar things feel unbearably important for one night.

## Closer

If the family makes it to morning, the final feeling is not victory. It is relief. The night passes, and one small line about biryani can land harder than any dramatic speech.
